SDR Intelligence Framework
Account Research Playbook
Before first outreach, spend 20–30 minutes per account building an intelligence profile. The goal is to enter the conversation already knowing something they haven't told you.
Where to Look & What to Find
💼
LinkedIn — People Intelligence
  • Search "Head of EUC / Digital Workplace / End User Computing" at the target org
  • Check their post history — have they shared anything about AVD, Azure, or cloud desktops?
  • Look at their career history — prior experience at Citrix shops signals existing platform dependency
  • Check mutual connections for warm intro paths
  • Look at recent activity — conference attendance, tech posts, comments on Microsoft announcements
📢
Job Postings — Platform Signals
  • Search LinkedIn Jobs / Indeed for: "Azure Virtual Desktop", "AVD", "Windows 365", "Citrix", "VMware Horizon", "EUC", "Digital Workplace"
  • New AVD engineer hire = they have an AVD environment with no management tooling yet
  • Citrix admin role closing + AVD role opening = active migration conversation

  • Volume of EUC roles = team under capacity pressure
📰

SDR Call Framework
Cold Call Script — Cloud Desktop Platform
This isn't a word-for-word script. It's a framework. The goal of the call is one of three outcomes: discovery conversation, meeting booked, or intelligence gathered. All three are wins.
Opening — First 10 Seconds
"Hi [Name], it's [Your Name] from Nerdio. I'll be brief — I work with EUC and digital workplace teams managing cloud desktop environments at [FSI/retail] organisations in the UK. I wanted to reach out because [personalised hook — job posting / Azure news / Citrix context]. Is now a bad time for 2 minutes?"
Key: lead with relevance, not your company. "Is now a bad time" outperforms "Do you have a minute" — it's respectful and gets a genuine response.
Bridge — If They Stay On
"Thanks — I'll be quick. I've been speaking to a number of [banks / insurers / retailers] this year about how they're managing Azure Virtual Desktop environments at scale. The consistent theme is that the operational overhead — image management, autoscaling, governance — creates more work than most teams anticipated. Does that resonate with what you're seeing at [Company]?"
Pause after this. Let them answer. The answer — however short — is intelligence you can use.
Discovery Question 1 — Platform Reality
"Can I ask — what does your current cloud desktop estate look like? Are you predominantly AVD, Citrix, VMware, or a mix? And is that under active review?"
Don't rush past their answer. If they say "we're mid-Citrix review" — that's the whole conversation now.
Discovery Question 2 — Operational Pain
"And for the team managing that environment — what's taking the most time? I'm curious whether it's the day-to-day management overhead, the cost of running the Azure infrastructure, or something else entirely."
This question surfaces the specific pain point that determines which Nerdio capability to lead with in the meeting.
Meeting Invitation
"Based on what you've shared — it sounds like [reflect back their specific pain]. We've helped a number of [similar organisations] address exactly that. I'd like to set up 30 minutes with you and [AE name] to walk through how — and be honest if it's not a fit. Are you free [offer two specific days and times]?"
Reflect their language back to them. "We've helped organisations like [Company]" is more persuasive than "let me tell you about our product."
Objection: "We're happy with how we manage it"
Response
"That's great to hear — and I'm not going to tell you there's a problem where there isn't one. A lot of the teams I speak to said the same thing before they actually quantified what AVD management was costing them in engineering time and compute spend. The 30 minutes is just to validate that — if the numbers don't stack up, I'll tell you."
Objection: "We're locked into Citrix / contract"
Response
"That's a very common position right now. Most of the conversations we're having aren't about moving this week — they're about understanding what the landscape looks like so when the contract comes up, you're not making a reactive decision. That's honestly the most valuable 30 minutes I can offer you."
Objection: "Send me some information"
Response
"I can do that — but I'd rather send you something specific to your situation rather than a brochure. Can I ask two quick questions so I can make sure what I send is actually relevant? [Go to Discovery Question 1]"
Meeting Design
How to Position the First Meeting
Never position the first meeting as "a demo of Nerdio." Frame it as a conversation about their world — with Nerdio as the context. The meeting should feel like a briefing, not a sales call.
Meeting Themes — Choose the Most Relevant
Theme 1 — Operational Complexity
"How UK Enterprises Are Operating Azure Virtual Desktop at Scale"
Framed as a peer briefing — what we're seeing across the UK EUC market, how organisations at similar scale are managing AVD environments, and where the operational gaps most commonly appear. We'll show you how leading teams have addressed them — and let you tell us if any of it maps to [Company]'s situation.
Best for: Any account running AVD or actively evaluating it. EUC architects and Heads of EUC.
Theme 2 — Migration Momentum
"Citrix to AVD in Financial Services — Lessons from Early Movers"
Specifically designed for FSI accounts in a Citrix strategy conversation. We'll walk through how similar firms have approached the transition — what the decision looked like, how the operational model changed, and what the cost picture actually looked like 12 months in. No commitment required to attend.
Best for: FSI accounts with Citrix · Barclays, Lloyds, Aviva, Standard Chartered personas. Reference: Grant Thornton $20K/month saving.
Theme 3 — Cost Governance
"Why Azure Cloud Desktop Costs Are Higher Than They Need to Be"
A data-led session on where Azure compute spend leaks in AVD environments — idle VMs, over-provisioned host pools, manual scaling decisions made at 9am that don't account for the 11pm usage pattern. We'll run the numbers against what your estate might look like and show you what the savings opportunity is before asking for anything.
Best for: Any account with Azure investment and a cost optimisation agenda. CFO-adjacent personas. Finance and insurance sector.
Theme 4 — Windows 365 Adoption
"Windows 365 at Scale — What the Management Layer Looks Like"
Windows 365 is increasingly the right answer for a significant portion of workforce personas — especially contractors, seasonal workers, and branch staff. This session covers how organisations are structuring their W365 deployment alongside AVD, and what the management model looks like when you're managing both at scale.
Best for: Retail accounts (M&S, Tesco, ASOS) · Accounts with W365 pilot activity · Contractor-heavy workforce personas.
