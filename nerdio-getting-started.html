Getting Started

Your first week with the
Sales Command Centre

An interactive walkthrough that maps every tool to your sales workflow — so you know exactly what to use, when to use it, and why it matters.

Step 1 — Territory Preparation
Know Your Territory
Before you pick up the phone, you need to understand the landscape. Who are the target accounts in your patch? What signals suggest they're ready for Nerdio? Start by building a picture of your territory and the accounts within it.
🏢
Account Intelligence
Primary tool for this stage
Your account database with heat scoring, sector filters, and territory views. Browse Tier 1/2/3 accounts, check 6sense intent signals, review Citrix and VMware exposure flags, and add your own notes as you research each account.
Pro tip: Start with the Tier 1 — Flagship tab. These 30 accounts are your highest-priority targets. Use the sector filters (FSI / Retail) to focus your first day.

Pro tip: Use the Research Playbook and Triggers tabs to understand what makes an account "ready" before you start outreach.

Step 2 — Competitive Intelligence
Learn the Competitive Landscape
Every deal has a competitive context. Your prospects are running Citrix, VMware/Omnissa, native Azure, or some combination. Understanding how Nerdio positions against each is essential before any conversation.
🎯
Competitive Battlecards
Primary tool for this stage
Side-by-side competitive positioning for Citrix, VMware/Omnissa, and Native Azure. Covers feature comparisons, discovery questions that expose pain, objection handling, proof points, landmines to plant, and ready-to-use talk tracks.
Pro tip: Don't just memorise features. Focus on the "Landmines to Set" and "Discovery Questions" sections — these are what shift a conversation in your favour.
Open Battlecards →

Journey Models — Customer Journeys
Strategic context
The Customer Journeys tab lets you compare up to 3 journeys side-by-side — both AS-IS migration paths (from Citrix, VMware, on-prem, native Azure) and TO-BE destinations (AVD, Windows 365, Hybrid, Intune). This gives you the full strategic picture of where a customer is today and where Nerdio takes them.
Pro tip: Select one AS-IS journey and one TO-BE destination to see how they map together. This becomes your narrative for the customer conversation.

Step 4 — Meeting Preparation
Prepare for Discovery Meetings
You've booked a meeting. Now make it count. These tools help you prepare the right questions for the right audience, understand where the customer sits on their cloud journey, and position Nerdio against their specific situation.
💬
Discovery Question Engine
Primary tool for this stage
50+ persona-targeted discovery questions filtered by stakeholder role (CIO, IT Admin, Architect), competitive situation, product focus, and deal stage. Each question comes with coaching notes, "listen-for" signals, and Nerdio capability mapping.
Pro tip: Filter by the persona you're meeting. A CIO cares about cost governance and risk — an IT Admin cares about daily simplification. Different questions for different audiences.
Open Discovery Engine →
🗺️
Journey Models — Assessment Tool
Meeting framework
The Assessment Tool tab lets you score a customer across 10 maturity dimensions, then generates a radar chart showing their current state vs. target. This produces a gap analysis with prioritised Nerdio capabilities — a powerful meeting artefact.
Pro tip: Run the assessment collaboratively with the customer during the meeting. It positions you as a strategic advisor, not just a vendor, and gives you a natural framework for the conversation.

Step 5 — Building the Business Case
Build the Business Case
Discovery is done — now quantify the value. These tools help you build an ROI story, demonstrate cost savings, and create the financial justification that gets deals approved by finance and procurement.
🧮
ROI Calculator
Primary tool for this stage
Interactive ROI modelling across four value dimensions: Azure compute savings (auto-scaling), operational efficiency gains, license consolidation, and risk reduction. Shows ROI percentage, payback period, and 3/5-year projections with an exportable summary.
Pro tip: Lead with the auto-scaling compute savings — this is the most tangible, quantifiable benefit. Nerdio's patented auto-scaling delivers 55% average savings. Then layer on operational and license savings for the full picture.
Open ROI Calculator →
🗺️
Journey Models — Maturity Model
Strategic narrative
The Maturity Model maps the customer's journey from Level 1 (Manual/Legacy) to Level 5 (Autonomous). Use this to frame the business case not just as a cost saving, but as a transformation story — "here's where you are, here's where you need to be, and here's the path."
Pro tip: Executives respond to maturity models. It shifts the conversation from "buy our product" to "accelerate your transformation."
Open Journey Models →
Step 6 — Deal Management
Manage Your Pipeline
You have deals in motion. Now manage them with discipline. These tools provide deal tracking, MEDPIC qualification, reverse timeline planning, and pipeline visibility — so nothing falls through the cracks.
⏱️
Deal Reverse Timeline Builder
Primary tool for this stage
Enter a target close date and complexity level — the tool reverse-engineers a complete deal timeline across 7 phases with MEDPIC qualification at every stage. Includes Nerdio positioning guidance, partner tasks, coaching alerts, and deal health scoring.
Pro tip: Build the timeline as soon as a deal enters pipeline. Work backwards from the close date to ensure you have time for every critical step — especially technical validation and procurement. Deals that skip steps stall.
Open Timeline Builder →
📊
Deal Pipeline Dashboard
Pipeline overview
Visual pipeline with MEDPIC scores, deal status tracking, activity feeds, and summary statistics. See your entire opportunity set at a glance — deals by stage, by score, by partner status. Identify which deals need attention and where to focus your week.
Pro tip: Review the dashboard every Monday morning. Sort by MEDPIC score to find deals that are under-qualified. A deal with a low score needs more discovery, not more demos.
Open Pipeline Dashboard →

1
Explore your territory in Account Intelligence
▼

Open Account Intelligence and browse the Tier 1 accounts. Use the FSI and Retail filters to understand your patch. Check each account's heat score, Citrix/VMware exposure, and any existing notes. Add your own research notes as you go — they auto-save to your browser.

If you have your own account data, use the Data Sources tab to import from Excel or CSV.

2
Study the Competitive Battlecards
▼

Open Competitive Battlecards and read through all three competitors: Citrix, VMware/Omnissa, and Native Azure. Focus on the Discovery Questions and Landmines sections — these will shape how you lead conversations.

Key narrative: Nerdio isn't "cheaper Citrix" — it's a fundamentally different, cloud-native approach. You're not selling a product, you're selling a simpler architecture.

3
Understand the Customer Journeys
▼

Open Journey Models and go to the Customer Journeys tab. Select one AS-IS migration path (e.g., "From Citrix DaaS") and one TO-BE destination (e.g., "To Azure Virtual Desktop"). This pairing becomes your story for that account type.

Then check the Assessment Tool tab — practise scoring a hypothetical account across the 10 dimensions. This is a powerful tool for customer meetings.

4
Build your first outreach cadence
▼

Pick your top 5 Tier 1 accounts. For each, open the SDR Pipeline Engine and identify their primary trigger event (Citrix EOL, VMware/Broadcom, Azure cost pressure, etc.).

Then use the Email Sequence tab for a multi-touch cadence, the LinkedIn tab for social touches, and the Call Script tab for phone outreach. Layer in ready-made templates from Outreach Sequences.

5
Prep for your first discovery meeting
▼

When you book a meeting, open Discovery Question Engine and filter by the persona you're meeting (CIO, IT Director, Architect, etc.). Pick 5-7 questions that match their likely pain points.

Review the coaching notes and "listen-for" signals — these tell you what a positive response sounds like and how to pivot to Nerdio capabilities. Go in with questions, not a pitch.

6
Set up deal tracking for active opportunities
▼

Once a deal enters pipeline, create a reverse timeline in the Deal Reverse Timeline Builder. Enter the target close date and complexity level — the tool will generate your deal plan with MEDPIC milestones at every stage.

Then add the deal to the Pipeline Dashboard for ongoing tracking. Review every Monday: which deals have low MEDPIC scores? Those need more discovery, not more demos.

7
Build the business case with ROI modelling
▼

When a deal progresses to business case stage, open the ROI Calculator. Model the customer's expected savings across four dimensions: Azure compute (auto-scaling), operational efficiency, license consolidation, and risk reduction.

Lead with the compute savings — Nerdio's patented auto-scaling delivers 55% average savings. The calculator produces an exportable summary you can share with the economic buyer.
