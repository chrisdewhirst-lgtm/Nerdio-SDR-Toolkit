Chapter 1

Getting Started

The Nerdio Sales Command Centre is a browser-based toolkit that gives SDRs, AEs, and Managers a unified set of sales tools — from territory management and outreach through to deal execution and coaching. Everything runs locally in your browser with data stored per-user, so your pipeline, territory, and activity are private to you.

What You Need

A modern browser (Chrome, Edge, Firefox, or Safari) is all that's required. No installation, no server, no VPN. Simply open the index.html file and log in. All data is stored in your browser's local storage, meaning your accounts, settings, and progress persist between sessions on the same device.

Important — Browser Data

Because everything is stored locally, clearing your browser data or switching devices will reset your session. If you work across multiple machines, you'll need to re-upload your territory data on each one.

Chapter 2

Your First Login

Open the Command Centre

Navigate to the index.html file. If this is the very first time anyone has opened the system, you'll be prompted to set an admin master password. This password protects the Admin Panel where users are created.

Get Your Credentials

Your manager or admin will create your account via the Admin Panel and provide your username and initial password. Each account is assigned a role: SDR, AE, or Manager.

Sign In

Enter your username and password on the login screen. On successful login, you'll see the role selection screen showing the portals available to you based on your role.

Enter Your Portal

Click your portal card to enter your home base. SDRs will go to "Pipeline Builder", AEs to "Deal Closer", and Managers to "Team Leader". You'll also see the Enablement Hub link which is available to everyone.

Complete Setup

Each portal has an initial setup section where you upload your territory data, select verticals, and set targets. Complete this step to personalise your tools.

Chapter 3

SDR Pipeline Builder

The SDR Portal is your daily operating hub for prospecting, running outreach sequences, qualifying leads, and handing off meetings to AEs. Everything is built around your territory — the accounts you own and the pipeline you generate.

Initial Setup

When you first enter the SDR Portal, you'll see a three-step setup wizard at the top of the page:

Upload Territory Data

Drag and drop or click to upload a CSV or Excel file containing your accounts. The system reads columns like account name, vertical/industry, priority, status, size, and revenue. Once uploaded, this data feeds into the Account Intelligence and CRM tools automatically.

Select Your Verticals

Click the vertical chips that match your territory focus — Financial Services, Healthcare, Education, Legal, Manufacturing, Public Sector, Professional Services, Retail, Technology, or Media. This helps personalise outreach templates and discovery questions.

Set Pipeline Target

Enter your monthly qualified pipeline target (in currency). This powers the progress tracking on your stats bar and helps you see at a glance how close you are to target.

Your Stats Bar

Across the top of your portal, five key metrics keep you oriented: Accounts (total in territory), Active Sequences (running outreach), Meetings Booked, Pipeline Generated ($), and Target Progress (%). These update as you work with the tools.

Portal Tabs

Your portal has three tabs:

My Tools (default)

Your grid of 8 curated tools covering pipeline management, outreach, discovery, battlecards, and account intelligence. Each card shows the tool name, a brief description, and a category tag (Core, Intelligence, or Enablement).

Territory

Your account data management view. See all uploaded accounts in a searchable table, add accounts manually, refresh/re-upload your data, or clear everything and start fresh. You can also filter and search by name, vertical, or status.

Activity

A timestamped feed of your recent actions — logins, data uploads, tool usage. Useful for tracking your own workflow and reviewing what you did in previous sessions.

Remove all uploaded territory data and return tools to their default state. Useful when changing territories or starting a new quarter.

Chapter 5

Manager Team Leader

The Manager Portal gives you full visibility across your team with oversight tools, coaching views, and reporting. You can also "view as" any team member to see exactly what they see in their portal — including their uploaded territory data and tool state.

Your Stats Bar

Six team-level metrics: Team Size, Active Deals, Total Pipeline ($), Forecast ($), Deals at Risk, and Target Progress (%).

The View-As Feature

This is one of the most powerful Manager capabilities. From the role selection screen, when you click on the SDR or AE portal card, you'll see a team member picker instead of going directly to the portal. Select a team member to view their portal exactly as they see it — with their data, their stats, and their activity.

How View-As Works

When viewing as a team member, a banner appears at the top of their portal showing who you're viewing as and a dropdown to switch to a different team member. All data shown is that team member's actual data (territory uploads, activity, stats). This is view-only — you're seeing their perspective without modifying anything. To return to your own Manager portal, use the "Switch Role" link or navigate back to the role selection screen.

Portal Tabs

Overview

Team pipeline table showing every rep's active deals, pipeline value, target progress, and health status. Below that, a "Deals Closing This Quarter" timeline with deal cards. Your at-a-glance team view.

Team Territory

Aggregated view of all team members' uploaded territory data. Filter by team member, role (SDR/AE), or search for specific accounts. Summary cards show total accounts, reps with data uploaded, and SDR vs AE account breakdowns.

Coaching

Three-column layout organising tools into Discovery Review (whiteboards, discovery questions), Deal Review (timelines, ROI, journey maps), and Intelligence (account intel, pipeline, enablement). Use these during 1:1s and deal reviews.

Tools

Grid view of all tools available to managers, tagged as Oversight, Coaching, or Intelligence. Click any tool to open it with full access to team data.

Reports

Four report cards covering Pipeline Summary, Team Performance, Deal Health, and SDR Metrics. Each card has key data points. Use the "Export Summary" button to generate a shareable report.

💡

Day-to-day workflow: Start each morning on the Overview tab to check pipeline health and deal status. Before 1:1s, use the View-As feature to see the rep's portal and prepare coaching points. During pipeline reviews, switch to Reports for data-backed discussion. Use the Coaching tab to run structured deal reviews with your team.

Data Import & Upload

Territory data is the fuel that powers the intelligence tools. Uploading your accounts from a CSV or Excel file means the Account Intelligence and CRM tools show your real accounts instead of sample data.

Supported Formats

FormatExtensionsNotes
CSV.csvComma-separated values. Most CRM exports support this.
Excel.xlsx, .xlsReads the first sheet. Column headers should be in row 1.

Expected Columns

The system is flexible and will map common column names automatically. The more columns you provide, the richer your tool experience will be:

ColumnRequired?Example Values
Account Name / Name / CompanyYesBarclays, NHS Trust, etc.
Vertical / Industry / SectorRecommendedFinancial Services, Healthcare
Priority / TierRecommendedHigh, Medium, Low or Tier 1, 2, 3
StatusOptionalProspecting, Engaged, Qualified, Negotiation
Revenue / ARROptional50000, 250000
Size / EmployeesOptional500, 5000, 50000
Notes / CommentsOptionalFree text
Last Activity / Last ContactOptional2025-01-15

How to Upload

Prepare Your File

Export your account list from your CRM (Salesforce, HubSpot, Dynamics, etc.) as a CSV or Excel file. Make sure the first row contains column headers.

Go to Your Portal

SDRs: Enter the SDR Portal and look for the "Upload Territory Data" card in the setup section, or switch to the Territory tab. AEs: Enter the AE Portal and use the setup section upload area.

Upload the File

Drag your file onto the upload area, or click to browse. The system will parse it immediately and show you a count of accounts loaded.

Verify

Check the Territory tab (SDR) or the Account Data bar (AE) to confirm the correct number of accounts was imported. Open the Account Intelligence or CRM tool to see your accounts in action.

Re-uploading & Refreshing Data

When your territory changes or you get a fresh CRM export, you don't need to clear everything first — just use the re-upload flow:

Refresh / Re-upload

Click the "Refresh / Re-upload Data" button in the Territory tab (SDR) or the Account Data bar (AE). The system will ask you to confirm before replacing your existing data. Select your new file and it will overwrite the old accounts. All tools that use territory data will update automatically.

Clear All Data

Click "Clear All" to remove all uploaded account data and return the intelligence tools to their default sample state. You'll be asked to confirm before anything is deleted. This is useful for quarterly resets or territory changes.

How Data Flows Through Tools

Once uploaded, your territory data automatically appears in two key tools:

ToolWhat It UsesHow It Transforms
Account Intelligence UKIName, Vertical, Priority, Size, Revenue, Status, NotesMaps to UKI format with segments, industry tags, and signal tracking
Account Intelligence CRMName, Vertical, Priority, Status, RevenueMaps to CRM tiers (Priority → Tier), with default scoring and tracking fields
💡

Best practice: Upload a fresh CRM export at the start of each week or quarter. The more complete your data (including status, revenue, and notes), the richer the intelligence tools become. Even a simple CSV with just account names and verticals is valuable — you can always enrich it later.

Manager View-As — Deep Dive

The View-As feature lets managers see any team member's portal exactly as that person sees it — with their uploaded data, their stats, and their tool state. This is essential for coaching, pipeline reviews, and understanding each rep's perspective.

Go to Role Selection

From the Command Centre home screen (or click "Switch Role" from any portal), you'll see all three portal cards.

Click SDR or AE Portal

As a Manager, instead of entering the portal directly, you'll see a team member picker showing all active users with that role.

Select a Team Member

Click on the person you want to view as. The system stores this selection and opens their portal.

Review Their View

You'll see a banner at the top confirming you're viewing as that person. All data, stats, and tools reflect their perspective. Use the dropdown in the banner to switch to a different team member without going back to the role selection screen.

Return to Your Portal

Click "Switch Role" in the top bar to go back to the role selection screen and enter your own Manager Portal.

Chapter 11

Admin & User Management

The Admin Panel is where user accounts are created and managed. It's protected by a separate master password that's set on first launch.

First-Time Setup

The very first time anyone opens the Command Centre, the admin will be prompted to set a master password. This password is separate from any user login — it protects the admin panel only. Keep it secure.

Creating Users

Open the Admin Panel

Click "Admin Panel" from the bottom of the role selection screen and enter the master password.

Fill in User Details

Enter the person's full name, choose a username, set an initial password, and select their role (SDR, AE, or Manager).

Click "Add User"

The account is created immediately. Share the username and password with the new user so they can log in.

Managing Existing Users

ActionWhat It Does
DisablePrevents the user from logging in. Their data is preserved. Can be re-enabled later.
EnableReactivates a disabled account.
Reset PasswordSets a new password for the user. You'll need to share it with them.
DeletePermanently removes the user account. Their uploaded data in localStorage is not automatically cleared.

Changing the Admin Password

At the bottom of the Admin Panel, there's a "Change Admin Password" section. Enter and confirm your new password to update it. This only affects the admin panel login — user passwords are separate.

FAQ & Troubleshooting

My uploaded data isn't showing in the Account Intelligence tool
Make sure you uploaded from within your portal (not the admin panel). The tools look for territory data stored under your specific username. Try opening the tool directly from your portal's tool grid — the auth guard passes your user identity to the tool.
I cleared my browser data and lost everything
All Command Centre data is stored in browser localStorage. If you clear site data, cookies, or use a private/incognito window, your data won't be available. Keep a copy of your territory CSV so you can re-upload if needed.
I can see the SDR Portal but not the AE Portal
Portal visibility is role-based. SDRs only see the SDR Portal. If you need access to AE tools, ask your admin to update your role in the Admin Panel.
My CSV upload seems to import but shows 0 accounts
Check that your CSV has a header row with recognisable column names. The system looks for columns like "name", "account", "company" for account names. If your columns use unusual names, try renaming them to standard labels before uploading.
As a manager, the View-As dropdown is empty
The picker only shows active users. If no SDRs or AEs have been created in the Admin Panel (or they're all disabled), the list will be empty. Create user accounts first via Admin.
How do I switch between roles?
Click "Switch Role" in the top bar of any portal to return to the role selection screen. From there, click the portal card for the role you want to enter. Managers can access all portals; AEs can access SDR and AE; SDRs can only access SDR.
Can I use this on my phone or tablet?
The tools are designed for desktop browsers. While some pages may render on mobile, the full experience — especially whiteboards and complex tools — works best on a laptop or desktop screen.
What data formats does the Excel upload support?
Both .xlsx and .xls formats are supported via the SheetJS library. The system reads the first sheet and uses row 1 as column headers. Multiple sheets are not merged — only the first is used.
How do I share the Command Centre with my team?
Share the v2 folder with your team via a shared drive, SharePoint, or any file sharing method. Each person opens index.html in their own browser. User accounts and data are stored per-browser, so each team member has their own private workspace.
Can multiple people log in with the same account?
Technically yes (on different devices), but each browser has its own localStorage, so they won't share uploaded data or activity history. It's better to create individual accounts for each person.
